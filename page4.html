The Battle of Maelshyve's Fortress

This planet stirs, a tiny place, Divinely come to flower.
Life grows upon a mote in space — that Demons would devour.
A tendril fell and then took root, by Maelshyve's dire caress.
In Zaulfung Swamp it came to fruit, and evil coalesced.

The Anduwen Watch would not sit by and watch her propagation.
They knew the price of letting lie was ultimate damnation.
They planned a raid into the heart of murkiness and mire
Knowing from the very start they'd earn her bitter ire.

Saragos, Mazrian, Seldaren, Qiyan made up the forward force.
Descending down, they traveled on to set the battle's course.
By the bridge they settled in to face the Necromants
In the dark would they begin with sword and fierce incants.

And Maltris came to fend the rope and keep it from their feet.
The cultists had but little hope; they'd climb and he'd unseat.
Every time they'd try the pass he'd leap off from the summit
To catch himself upon their mass — and both of them would plummet.

The army met in Riverhaven, the holy Temple filled,
And Mother Whiteburn, blessed maven, our piety distilled.
With arms upraised, she spoke of war, in Hodierna's prayer,
To gird our souls in light before we'd plunge into that lair.

What did I see then, standing tall, aglow with holy light?
Could it be, She heard our call, and came with us to fight?
Yes, listener, you won't believe the sight that I then spied
A Unicorn joined us that eve, to battle by our side.

Once below, I blew the horn to keep our party well,
That bell-like sound to strictly warn away the hounds of hell.
Perune in front, we traveled through the murky cavern pass
The darkness loomed, all light eschewed, within the vile morass.

We met the bridge and huddled there, our party well impeded
As only one could cross, with care, the rest of us conceded.
SHE whispered then inside our minds — I shivered down my back.
But she would grow, and crush, and grind, until our skulls would crack.

I blinked away that dire scream as blood ran from my eyes
And bellowed that the demon's scheme would end in her demise.
Around us then were bloody wraiths and shylvics breathing ice,
So to the test we put our faith and paid the battle price.

The unicorn reared and rushed ahead, its pearly horn ablaze,
And speared the foes with running head and scorching, holy graze.
It spared no rival of the good and cut down the Perverse
Who dared to ply their damned falsehoods and shore the demon's curse.

At last we made it 'cross the rift and built our first redoubt,
The healers wielding their fair gift, bearing all throughout.
I saw Szrael, Zymi, Aislynn, Kaelie, Qiyan, and Karthor
Shaking from the pain within, yet wading through the gore.

Ahead we found the demon's knight, a woman turned afoul,
Her skin all scaled with dusky blight, her voice a screeching howl.
She cried out then, 'AT LAST, SOME SPORT!' and fell upon our man,
Her plated wings in arched support, a great and chilling span.

Sir Samsaren, his axe held high, he led the forward charge
And Ciriasa leapt to fly and flailed into his targe.
She roared in fury, snapped her wings, and pushed him to the ground,
Then carved from him some fleshy strings and made a crowing sound.

But still he held, he held and held, knowing all the while
That for the demon to be felled we'd have to use our guile.
So while he held, the others stood and moved to find their stake
All praying that our tactic could the demoness unmake.

They each had vials, glowing soft, which held the healing tonic
And if they could but send aloft would cure the one cthonic.
But as they threw, she pitched and yawed to make them miss their mark
And cackled as she bit and clawed, our chances growing dark.

Sir Samsaren then set his gaze and drew up all his might
His magic put her in a daze and nullified her flight.
She crashed aground, now brought low, awash in her own bile.
Seldaren made the final blow — he nailed her with a vial.

We all rejoiced, oh glory day, we'd brought the demon down!
We showed her that our mortal way could keep her from her crown.
And so we fend our tiny place, Divinely brought to flower,
And prove to all, in this one case, the demon won't devour.
